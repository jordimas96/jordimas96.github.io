Childhood

Since I was a child, I have always been fascinated by any kind of technology. From the age of 4, I used to spend whole afternoons playing and building Bionicles, a line of articulated LEGO figures, often without looking at the instructions; I knew how to put them together just by looking at the final result. When I took them apart, I used the pieces to invent new ones. I would see an image on the internet of a new Bionicle and try to build it, improvising with the pieces I had available, often quite successfully. Due to this, I developed a very strong spatial vision and great observation capacity from a very young age.

When I grew up and had access to a screwdriver, I would open up every device that broke to try to fix it. Something wasn't broken until I opened it up and looked at it, with or without YouTube tutorials.

I always wanted to be a video game developer, and in high school I studied an introduction to HTML and other tools and I liked it so much that I decided to study programming. I studied 2 higher cycles and over time I have specialized in the frontend part, although I have often had to intervene in all aspects of programming.
In high school I was very good at technical drawing, technology, electrical engineering, physics and philosophy.

Skills

  • Logical and rational thinking
  • Great observation skills
  • Sharp intuition and quick analysis of problems to deduce the best solution
  • Creativity and innovative thinking, tendency to think outside the box
  • Taste for graphic design, styles, shapes and colors. Consistency of design
  • Constant concern that motivates me to always look for ways to improve/automate/optimize/simplify processes. Both in computing and in my daily life
  • High ability to concentrate when a topic fascinates me

I have a quick intuition to anticipate results based on patterns, unconsciously. I can often analyze and discard possible solutions and intuit in the moment what will be the best solution, before I myself realize how I did it.

Values in programming

  • Empathy with the user and ergonomic development
  • Always responsive development by default
  • Attractive, intuitive and ergonomic interfaces to provide the most pleasant user experience possible at all times.
  • When the user performs an action or analyzes the page, do not produce unexpected or counterintuitive results
  • Do not limit the user without reason
  • Follow good practices in design and code
  • Excellence lies in the details. Perfectionist
  • Development taking into account possible future changes
  • Creativity and always trying new things
