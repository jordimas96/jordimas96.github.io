Descripció del projecte
Principal desenvolupador frontend d'un backoffice per al Ministerio de Asuntos Exteriores y Cooperación, destinat a ajudar consolats i ambaixades a oferir suport als ciutadans espanyols que es troben a l'estranger durant situacions de crisi, com ara atemptats terroristes o catàstrofes naturals.
L'aplicació permet gestionar grups de ciutadans per a organitzar trasllats, salconduits, repatriacions, renovació de documents, comunicacions i atenció a necessitats especials, entre d'altres serveis.

Funcionalitats principals
L'aplicació és una Single Page Application desenvolupada amb Angular. Inclou diverses seccions per crear i gestionar crisis, afegir afectats i evacuats, així com els seus acompanyants i persones de contacte. Permet gestionar els documents dels afectats i portar un registre de comunicacions, tant individuals com en grup, i crear i seguir protocols d'actuació específics per a cada crisi.
Té mapes amb la zona afectada que s'està gestionant, amb ubicacions i enllaços interactius. Els mapes, taules i formularis d'edició tenen enllaços per navegar ràpidament per tota la web i accedir directament a les seccions. També disposa de sistema de gestió d'alertes i permet enviar comunicacions massives per correu electrònic, SMS i notificacions push.

Tecnologia i accessibilitat
Utilitza un sistema de stores i subscriptions per gestionar l'estat i emmagatzemar dades en caché, especialment útil en zones amb poca cobertura, habituals en situacions d'emergència. També sincronitza les dades i permet accedir als registres d'afectats provinents de tres fonts diferents del ministeri. Mostra mapes amb ubicacions i enllaços, i també té diversos enllaços per a accedir a seccions més ràpidament i directament.
A més, compleix tots els estàndards d'accessibilitat de nivell AA i compta amb suport per a diversos idiomes: castellà, anglès, català, euskera, gallec, asturià i francès.

Estructura i estils
L'estructura de la pàgina és semblant a la de les altres webs oficials de l'estat. Els estils estan completament desacoblats i organitzats. L'aplicació utilitza la llibreria Dintel, part de la qual la vaig desenvolupar jo mateix, per als seus components i estructures.

Equip i rol

Al cap de quatre mesos vaig cedir el rol de cap de projecte a un perfil sènior amb àmplia trajectòria a l'empresa, i vaig continuar com a desenvolupador frontend principal. Posteriorment, l'equip es va ampliar amb dos programadors més, que van anar canviant segons la càrrega de treball, mantenint l'equip entre tres i quatre membres en total.

Project Description
Lead frontend developer of a backoffice for the Ministry of Foreign Affairs and Cooperation, aimed at helping consulates and embassies provide support to Spanish citizens abroad during crisis situations, such as terrorist attacks or natural disasters.
The application allows managing groups of citizens to organize transfers, travel permits, repatriations, document renewals, communications, and attention to special needs, among other services.

Main Features
The application is a Single Page Application developed with Angular. It includes several sections to create and manage crises, add affected and evacuated people, as well as their companions and contacts. It allows managing affected people's documents and keeping a record of communications, both individual and group, and creating and following specific action protocols for each crisis.
It has maps with the affected area being managed, with locations and interactive links. Maps, tables, and editing forms have links to quickly navigate the web and access sections directly. It also has an alert management system and allows sending mass communications via email, SMS, and push notifications.

Technology and Accessibility
It uses a stores and subscriptions system to manage state and cache data, especially useful in low-coverage areas, common in emergencies. It also synchronizes data and allows access to affected people records from three different ministry sources. It shows maps with locations and links and also has several links to access sections more quickly and directly.
Additionally, it meets all level AA accessibility standards and supports multiple languages: Spanish, English, Catalan, Basque, Galician, Asturian, and French.

Structure and Styles
The page structure is similar to other official state websites. Styles are fully decoupled and organized. The application uses the Dintel library, part of which I developed myself, for its components and structures.

Team and Role
I started the project as the frontend team lead, consisting of three people. After four months, I passed the project lead role to a senior profile with extensive experience in the company and continued as lead frontend developer. Later, the team expanded with two more programmers, who rotated according to workload, keeping the team between three and four members in total.

Client Feedback
When the final demo was shown before delivery, the client especially highlighted how polished and well-made the application was.

Achievements

  • Frontend lead and main developer of the project, from scratch to delivery
  • Development of the application following a very strict style guide
  • In a short time, I became the reference developer for CSS and layout within the team and the project

Team and Collaboration
Shortly after joining the company, I became part of a team of 5 developers with whom we created an Angular component library, using PrimeNG, Angular Material, and other plugins as a base.

Library Content and Functionality
The library consists of 44 reusable components, from basic forms and UI elements (inputs, selectors, breadcrumbs) to advanced components such as menus, interactive maps, autocompletable selectors connected to Google, toasts, and common utilities for the ministry's applications.

Styles and Design
Components are developed following strictly defined styles, with every aspect of the design (colors, shapes, spacing, fonts, sizes, animations) strongly enforced.

Maintenance and Further Development
During subsequent projects at the company, I occasionally returned to this library to make changes and also develop new components, such as an interactive map where crisis areas, information points, and evacuation routes could be marked. I also developed an autocompletable location selector using data from a Google API.

Achievements

  • Participation in the creation and layout of the component library used in all official ministry websites
  • Development of several improvised components for a specific project that were so well received that, although not originally designed for the library, they became part of it and were adopted directly in several official ministry applications
  • Creation of an interactive map with multiple viewing modes, color markers, optional clusters, and routes. The code anticipates what you want and does it by default
