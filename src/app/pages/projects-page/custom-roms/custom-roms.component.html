Custom ROM devices

Samsung Galaxy Ace 2
Official support: Android 2.3
Installed Android 9. It had no official updates available, but I extended it to 6 years of support.
Samsung Galaxy S4
Official support: Android 4.2.2-5.0
Very compatible device, I installed up to Android 12, some TouchWiz versions, and One UI 7. It had 2 years of official support but I extended it to 9 years. Used also as an IR blaster.
Nexus 5X
Official support: Android 6-8
I installed up to Android 12.1, and MIUI. It had 2 years of official support, I extended it to 6 years.
OnePlus 6
Official support: Android 8-11
Installed Android 13, AOSP, PixelExperience. It had 3 years of official support, I extended it to 5 years. I could and wanted to continue (withi MIUI, One UI and further Android versions) but the UFS chip failed.
Xiaomi Redmi 6
Official support: Android 8-9
Installed Android 10 AOSP, extending the official support an extra year and getting a pure Android experience. It has a Mediatek processor, which is closed source, making custom ROM developement difficult.
Samsung Galaxy Ace
Official support: Android 2.2
Installed some TouchWiz ROMs, CyanogenMod, Android 5.
Xiaomi Pad 6
Official support: Android 13
Installed Android 14 AOSP to turn it into a lower-priced Pixel Tablet with a better display, performance and battery.
Xiaomi Redmi 12C
Official support: Android 12
Low-end device, no community support. I installed Android 13 via GSI to make it much more usable. It has a Mediatek processor, which is closed source, making custom ROM developement difficult.
Galaxy Tab A (2019)
Official support: Android 9-11
Installed Android 13 via LineageOS. It was a low-powered device with a heavy One UI layer, but with AOSP the performance improves, making it feel like new. With 1 year of official support, using custom roms I got up to 4 years of support.
